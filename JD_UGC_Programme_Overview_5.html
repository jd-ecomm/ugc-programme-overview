01 | THE ECONOMIC ENGINE
Why the programme works
The programme produces licensed creator content that runs on three channels. The key economic move is using Partnership Ads, where paid Meta media is delivered through the creator's own handle. That creative outperforms standard brand-handle creative on the same audience. The resulting ROAS uplift on existing media spend is the lever that funds the cost of producing the content. It does not happen instantly. There is a ramp.
The four-step logic
1
We pay creators to produce licensed content
Cost in. Cash outflow in months 1-6.
2
That content runs in Partnership Ads through the creator handle
It also runs on JD owned channels and on the creator's organic feed at no extra cost.
3
Partnership Ads beat brand-handle creative on the same audience
Working assumption: ~15% ROAS uplift on the portion of media that uses creator content. Pilot validates this.
4
The efficiency saving compounds and crosses programme cost
Break-even point: Month 7 at the modelled spend ramp. From month 8 the programme is net positive.

What this means for budget conversations. The programme is a net cost for the first six months. It needs to be funded from existing marketing budget through the ramp. Asking for net new budget assumes month-one breakeven, which the model does not support. Frame it as a redeployment of existing media spend toward more efficient creative.

05 | SCENARIO PICKER
Tell me about your scenario, I will tell you how to approach it
Answer a few questions to get a tailored recommendation. Useful for new co-op conversations, sizing an always-on engagement, or testing how the programme handles edge cases like sub-$5K budgets or extended usage requests.
What kind of activation are you planning?
This sets the baseline programme structure. Always-on uses 12-month usage rights and ongoing creators. Co-op is short and brand-partner funded.
What role will this creator play?
This sets the creator tier within the programme and shapes the contract.
What deliverable scope do you need?
This determines what we receive from the creator and what JD can use it for.
What budget is the brand partner offering?
Total co-op budget in CAD. This sets the spend ladder tier.
Is the brand contributing gifted product?
This is the hard prerequisite for the entry-tier economics. Without it, budget allocation has to shift.
Does the brand want extended usage rights?
Baseline is campaign-duration only. Extended rights post-campaign can be priced as an upsell. Open internal question on pricing model.
